Biography

I am currently an MD/PhD student in the Sidi Chen lab at Yale University. I develop and use a variety of modern tools including high-throughput CRISPR screening, in vivo gene editing and tumor modeling, immune engineering, systems-level profiling, and bioinformatics to study how molecular factors affect cancer development and anti-tumor immunity. My graduate school projects have spanned the development of methods for engineering more sophisticated chimeric antigen receptor T cells (CAR-Ts), screens for immunotherapy targets in melanoma and glioblastoma models, and analyses of immune correlates of COVID-19 pathogenesis. I have received a BS in Molecular Cellular and Developmental Biology with distinction from Yale in 2014 and have received the NCI Junior Investigator People’s Choice Award, NIH Postbac Intramural Research Training Award, Yale President’s Public Service Fellowship, and the Goldwater Scholarship. As an Air Force brat, I spent most of my childhood growing up in Japan and England. In my spare time I enjoy playing soccer, rooting for Ferrari in Formula 1, exploring new restaurants and museums, and spending time with friends and family.
